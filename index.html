A Small, Quaint Little House

I grew up in Rockford, Illinois. That was a small, quaint little house on the south side of Rockford.

I can picture it. Walking up to the screen-in front porch door, going into the door, taking a right, walking inside of the house. Straight ahead was my bedroom. To the right was the dining room.

There were six of us. My mom, my dad, myself, my brother, and my two sisters. I'm the oldest.

Why it looks like this

A keepsake, not a technology stunt.

Everything here is in service of one thing lasting a hundred years on a shelf. That's also why we hold a hard line on what we will never do.

  • i.

    We will never create a synthetic “clone,” voice double, or avatar of your parent. We preserve their voice — we do not imitate it.

  • ii.

    Your recordings are never sold, shared outside your family, or used to train any AI.

  • iii.

    Their words are arranged, never authored. Nothing in the book is invented; nothing is put in their mouth.
